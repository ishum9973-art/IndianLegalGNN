 MacKay, J. : These are the reasons for an Order dismissing an application for a stay of a deportation order issued against the applicant which was heard by telephone on an urgent basis on October 31, 1995.Counsel for both the applicant and the respondent were present at court premises in Toronto, with the court presiding from Ottawa.These reasons are filed pursuant to s. 51 of the Federal Court Act , R.S.C.1985, c. F-7, as amended.An application for leave and for judicial review, was filed on October 20, 1995 with reference to the decision on behalf of the Minister of Citizenship and Immigration, dated September 27, 1995 determining that the applicant is a person who constitutes a danger to the public in Canada and is, therefore, removable from Canada.Thereafter, on October 27, 1995 the applicant filed a notice of motion for a stay of a deportation order, pending the final determination of the application for leave and for judicial review.Deportation of the applicant to Iran was scheduled for November 1, 1995.The applicant, Karim Bavi, a citizen of Iran, had been a member of the Iranian Armed Forces, but deserted in 1987.After fleeing to Iraq and remaining in an Iraqi refugee camp for a time, the applicant entered and was granted landing in Canada on February 7, 1990, having been determined to be a Convention refugee overseas.In August 1991 the applicant was involved in two robberies for which he was convicted of two counts of robbery and one count of forceable confinement and he was sentenced to a total of four years and three months in prison.On August 18, 1993 a deportation order was issued against the applicant on the basis that he is a person described in s. 27(1)(d)(i) of the Immigration Act , R.S.C.1985, c. I-2 as amended (the " Act ") which provides, "27(1) An immigration officer or a peace officer shall forward a written report to the Deputy Minister setting out the details of any information in the possession of the immigration officer or peace officer indicating that a permanent resident is a person who  (d) has been convicted of an offence under any Act of Parliament for which a term of imprisonment of (i) more than six months has been imposed," An appeal of the deportation order was dismissed on January 23, 1995 by the Appeal Division of the Immigration and Refugee Board. The test established for determining whether the court should grant a stay of a removal order is that set out in Imm.L.R.The applicant was made aware of the concerns of the Minister and given opportunity to respond to them.
