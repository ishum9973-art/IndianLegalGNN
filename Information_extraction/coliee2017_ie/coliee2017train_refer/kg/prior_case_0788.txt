of the suit.Neither in the trial Court nor in the first appellate court any question of construction of any document or any question of drawing an inference of law arose.The questions which arose were simple questions of fact.Tire respondent appealed to the High Court and the appeal was heard by a single judge.Under the misconception that a judge is entitled in second appeal, to interfere with even concurrent findings of fact of the courts below not only where the said conclusions are based on no evidence but also where the said conclusions are based on evidence which the High Court considers insufficient to support them , the learned single judge examined the whole evidence and upset the concurrent findings of fact given by tire courts below.
