 The Bombay Government with the sanction of the Governor-General-in-Council imposed certain taxes in the cantonment of Poona including an entertainment tax of Rs. 10 per show  The impugned tax was distinguishable from a tax on a pro-fession or calling . It was a tax imposed on an actual show, and not on a profession or calling  Tax on entertainments was imposed in the Cantonment of Poona with effect from July 15, 1948 . The appellant paid the tax under protest and filed a suit against the respondent  The Court says the law is not only about taxes on luxuries, entertainments or amusements . It is a tax imposed on the act of entertainment resulting in a show . The Court  It may not be unreasonable or improper if a higher tax is imposed on the shows given by a cinema house which contains large seating accommodation and is situate in fashionable or busy localities .
