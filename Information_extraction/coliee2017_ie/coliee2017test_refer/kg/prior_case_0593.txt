574 A] In the present case the charge-sheet did not refer to or charge either of the accused with criminal conspiracy.The cognizance which the Magistrate took was therefore, only, of the offences alleged in the chargesheet, and it was only at the later stage of passing the committal order that he considered that a charge under s. 120B was more appropriate than that of abetment.[574 F-H] (2) Even on the basis that it is not the sections referred to in the charge-sheet that matter, but the offence prima facie disclosed by the allegations, in the present case the offence 'primarily and essentially disclosed in the charge- sheet and other documents was one of abetment of forgery and of the false impersonation.[575 F-H] Therefore, the Magistrate did not take cognizance of the offence under s. 120B, I.P.C., and hence, consent under s. 196A(2) Cr.P.C., was not a condition precedent.[576 B-C] CRIMINAL APPELLATE JURISDICTION: Criminal Appeal No.100 of 1969.572 Appeal by special leave from the judgment and order dated January 8, 1969 of the Bombay High Court in Criminal Application No.1341 of 1968.C.
