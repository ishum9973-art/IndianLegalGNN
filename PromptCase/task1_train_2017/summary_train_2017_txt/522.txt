 To nationalise the Saharanpur - Shahdara - Delhi route a draft scheme dated 26 th February, 1959 was published . Draft scheme published under old Act was quashed by the  Shri Chand etc. v. Govt. of U.P. over Jeevan Nath Bahl & Ors., v. State of . State of U .P.  Under Section 80 of the Act no private operator has right to apply for and obtain permits to ply the stage carriages on the approved or notified route/routes or areas or portion  The draft scheme dated 26th February, 1959 was published to nationalised Saharanpur - Shahdara - Delhi route . Approved scheme was quashed by the Allahabad High Court  Petition Nos. 9701/90, 9702/90 and 2083/91 arise against the High Court's Judgment dismissing the writ petitions in which grant of permits under s.80  The contention of Shri Harish Salve, the learned Senior counsel for contesting respondents, is that the scheme of nationalisation relates to "any area, route or portion thereof"  S. Abdul Khader Saheb v. Mysore Revenue Appellate Tribunal & Ors., [[ 1972] INSC 273; [[ 1973] 1 SCC 357 .  The Allahabad High Court relied on its earlier judgment and held that by operation of sub-sec. 4 of s.100 of the Act the draft scheme stood lapsed from one year  The fresh draft scheme dated February 13, 1986 had not been lapsed and would continue to be in operation . Protected protection was given only to 50 private operators including the appellants .
