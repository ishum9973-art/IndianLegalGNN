Held by the Court (PATANJALI SASTRI C.J., VlvIAN BosE, and GHULAM HASAN JJ.-MUKHERJEA and DAS JJ.dissenting)-The practice of law in India generally involves the exercise of both the functions of acting and pleading behalf of litigant parties, and when s. 2 of the abovesaid Act conferred upon an advocate of the Supreme Court the right to " practise " in any High Court, it is legitimate to understand that expression as authorising him to appear and plead as well as to act behalf of suitors in all the High Courts-including the Original Side thereof.It is fallacious to relate that expression as applied to an advocate either, the one band, to the court in which the advocate is enrolled, or,, the other, to the court in which he seeks to exercise the statutory right conferred him.It must be related to the general constitution of the Bar in India as a single agency in dealing with the litigant public.
