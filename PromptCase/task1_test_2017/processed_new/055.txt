and (b) that on the face of the complaint, it PG NO 455 PG NO 456 could not be said that the complaint spelts out the ingredients of the offence alleged.Hence these appeals by special leave by the appellant-State.Allowing the appeals to this Court HELD: (1) The orders of the High Court in Crl.Misc.223 of 1987 dated 13.2.1987 and the two orders in Crl.Misc.No.25X of 1987(R) and Crl.Misc.No.259 1987(R) dated 18.2.1987 are set aside and the order dated 1.7.1986 of the learned Magistrate taking cognizance of the offence and ordering issue of summons to the respondents is restored.The criminal case initiated on the complaint will now be proceeded with , in accordance with law.
